Trail Running Near Olivenhain

Ten trails from the front door to the worth-the-drive · Encinitas, North County San Diego

3376 Calle Tres Vistas sits in Olivenhain — one of the best-positioned addresses in coastal North County for trail running. A dense local network is on the doorstep, with several iconic San Diego trail systems a short drive away. What follows: ten trails grouped by how far you have to travel, plus practical notes on heat, water, and shared use.

Compiled from AllTrails, the City of Encinitas Open Space directory, Modern Hiker, and local trail maps · Distances are straight-line from home (driving distance is typically ~1.3× more) · Always check current conditions before heading out

01 · OLIVENHAIN

Olivenhain Loop (Brookside → Wildflower → Country Rose)

The de facto everyday Olivenhain run. Multi-use city trails through ranches and chaparral on an even surface — you can string the loop together right from your block, no driving, no parking, no fuss.

1.3 mi from home ~3.7 mi loop ~350 ft gain Mixed surface Shared with bikes
02 · OLIVENHAIN

Lone Hill Horse Trail / Trail 141

Quick-access singletrack with a steady, steep first quarter-mile that warms the legs up fast. The trail then bends and opens to canyon views. Best used as a short, sharp hill workout when you don't have an hour.

1.5 mi from home Short Steep opener Singletrack
03 · OLIVENHAIN

Wildflower Valley & Summit

Connects directly off the Olivenhain Loop. A marked horse trail with a long rolling straightaway past Fortuna Ranch — a clean, uninterrupted stretch that's ideal for tempo efforts and pickups.

1.8 mi from home Extendable Rolling Horse trail

Short drive

5–15 minutes — quick variety, different terrain

04 · CARDIFF

Manchester Preserve

123 acres, ~4 miles of trails. The main loop is around 2 miles but easily extendable. Rugged and steep in spots, and crucially pedestrian-only — no bikes or horses, so it runs quiet. Pair it with adjacent Oakcrest Park for the area's biggest local climb (~950 ft total gain) when you want a real workout.

3.3 mi from home ~2–4 mi Up to ~950 ft Rugged Pedestrian-only
05 · ENCINITAS

Indian Head Canyon (Quail Gardens Dr)

A 56-acre canyon that packs in everything from smooth wide paths to steep technical singletrack. Well-marked and loops back to the staging area, which makes it a great venue for variable-pace sessions — easy on the wide stuff, hammer the climbs.

3.1 mi from home Variable Mixed Smooth + technical Loops back
06 · CARDIFF

San Elijo Lagoon Ecological Reserve

Flat, fast, scenic lagoon-perimeter routes. The best summer option when the inland trails get hot — sea breeze, low elevation, and consistent footing. Multiple route options with strong AllTrails ratings across the board.

4.3 mi from home Flexible Flat Wide path Summer pick

Worth-the-drive destination runs

15–25 minutes — the iconic ones

07 · HARMONY GROVE

Elfin Forest Recreational Reserve

The big one nearby. The Way Up Trail climbs to the Lake Hodges Overlook (4.7★ over 3,700+ reviews — the highest-rated trail in the area). The full Elfin Forest Loop is ~4.5 mi / ~816 ft. Watch for heat April–November and rattlesnakes; early starts mandatory.

3.3 mi from home ~4.5 mi loop ~816 ft Singletrack Heat & snakes
08 · SAN MARCOS

Double Peak

Moderately challenging ~4.1 mile loop with the iconic final push to a 360° summit. The best skyline payoff in all of North County. There's no shade on the climb — go at sunrise or take the hit.

4.2 mi from home ~4.1 mi loop Climbing Exposed No shade
09 · SAN MARCOS

San Elijo Hills Loop (Lakeview Park)

The longest steady climb route in the immediate region — ~6.4 mi with ~1,300 ft of gain. The closest thing to a real long mountain run without driving all the way out to Mission Trails.

3.8 mi from home ~6.4 mi ~1,300 ft Sustained climb
10 · LAKE HODGES

Piedras Pintadas / Lake Hodges

An easy ~3.7 mile loop with interpretive signage, a creek crossing, and lake views. Perfect easy/recovery day or a shoulder-season option when the harder routes are too hot or too crowded.

7.9 mi from home ~3.7 mi loop Easy Wide path Recovery day

Practical Notes

Water

Almost none of these trails have water on-trail. Carry your own — Olivenhain especially, where the shade is good but water sources are absent.

Heat & snakes

Inland routes (Elfin Forest, Double Peak, San Elijo Hills) get serious heat and rattlesnakes from late April through early November. Early morning starts only.

Bikes

Olivenhain and Elfin Forest are heavily shared with mountain bikers. Manchester Preserve is the quiet, pedestrian-only option when you don't want to dodge bars.
