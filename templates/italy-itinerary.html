Italy: Como to Milan via Cinque Terre

A family hiking itinerary through Liguria · April 7–12, 2026

Six days from Lake Como to Milan, threading through the Ligurian coast with boots on the ground every day. Three kids, a car parked in La Spezia, and the full Cinque Terre trail split across two days — ending each hike at a beach or a bowl of pasta. Three route options depending on whether you want aquariums, cheese factories, or the Leaning Tower.

April 7–12, 2026 · 2 adults + 3 children · Car from Como, fly out of Milan

The Recommended Itinerary

A hybrid of the best elements: Genoa's old town on the way down, Cinque Terre hiked south-to-north ending at Monterosso's beach, Portovenere for a flex day, and the Portofino coastal trail on the way back to Milan. Every day has hiking, the driving never exceeds 3 hours, and the south-to-north direction means you finish at the only village with a real sandy beach.

April 7

Como → La Spezia via Genoa

  • Morning departure from Como. Drive A7 south to Genoa (~2 hrs)
  • Walk through Genoa's atmospheric old town alleyways (caruggi), grab focaccia al formaggio at Porto Antico
  • Continue on the A12 coast road to La Spezia (1.5 hrs). Park the car at Parcheggio Centro (~€15–18/day)
  • Evening walk along the La Spezia waterfront promenade. Get to bed early — big hiking days ahead
April 8

Cinque Terre Day 1: Riomaggiore → Corniglia

  • Train to Riomaggiore (8 min from La Spezia)
  • Hike Via dell'Amore to Manarola (~30 min, flat and scenic warm-up)
  • Gelato and photo break in Manarola's colorful harbor
  • Hike Manarola → Corniglia (~1.5 hrs, moderate with some stairs)
  • Explore Corniglia — the quiet hilltop village with stunning terrace views
  • Train back to La Spezia
~2–3 hrs hiking
April 9

Cinque Terre Day 2: Corniglia → Monterosso

  • Train to Corniglia
  • Hike Corniglia → Vernazza (~1.5–2 hrs — toughest and most rewarding section, steep switchbacks, cliff views)
  • Lunch in Vernazza — let the kids play on the small harbor beach
  • Hike Vernazza → Monterosso (~2 hrs, gorgeous descent into Monterosso)
  • Sandy beach finish — swim and celebrate completing all five villages!
  • Train back to La Spezia
~3.5–4 hrs hiking · All 5 villages done!
April 10

Portovenere & Palmaria Island

  • Short drive or bus to Portovenere (15 min) — stunning fortified village at the tip of the peninsula
  • Hike the Sentiero dell'Infinito (Trail of Infinity) — a ridge trail with panoramic views (~1.5 hrs)
  • Or: Ferry to Palmaria Island (5 min) and hike the island loop trail (~2–3 hrs). Rocky coves, far fewer tourists
  • Afternoon swim at Palmaria's beaches or back in Portovenere
  • Built-in flex: this can become a lazy beach day if anyone needs rest
~2–3 hrs hiking
April 11

Portofino Coastal Hike → Milan

  • Pick up the car, drive to Santa Margherita Ligure (~1.5 hrs up the coast)
  • Hike the Santa Margherita → Portofino coastal trail (~1.5 hrs, easy-moderate through Mediterranean pine forest)
  • Explore Portofino — the piazzetta, Castello Brown, maybe a swim at Paraggi beach
  • Ferry back to Santa Margherita (10 min — kids love the boat ride)
  • Drive to Milan area (~2.5 hrs). Optional: Duomo rooftop at sunset or dinner in Navigli
~1.5–3 hrs hiking
April 12

Fly Home

  • Morning departure from Milan (MXP or LIN)

The Three Options

Option A: Coastal Glamour

Genoa aquarium, south→north hike, Portofino, Milan

Start with Europe's largest aquarium in Genoa — a huge hit with kids. Two days hiking the Cinque Terre south-to-north, ending at Monterosso beach. Day 4 takes you to Portofino via ferry or coastal trail. Full day in Milan to close out — Duomo rooftop, Science Museum, or Parco Sempione.

Option B: Food Valley

Parma food tours, north→south hike, Portovenere, Pavia

Detour through Parma's "Food Valley" — visit a Parmigiano-Reggiano dairy, eat real Parma ham. Hike Cinque Terre north-to-south ending at Riomaggiore's dramatic harbor. Portovenere and Palmaria Island for a flex day, then the ornate Certosa di Pavia monastery on the way back to Milan.

Option C: Tuscan Detour

Lucca by bike, south→north hike, Pisa, Milan

Longer first day through Lucca — rent bikes and ride on top of the Renaissance city walls (4 km loop), climb the Torre Guinigi with oak trees on top. South-to-north Cinque Terre, then Pisa for the Leaning Tower and obligatory silly photos. Full day in Milan to finish.

Practical Notes

Cinque Terre Card required for trail access — includes unlimited trains between villages. Buy at any station or online. Family pricing available. With children, split the trail across 2 days. Hardest section: Vernazza ↔ Corniglia. Easiest: Via dell'Amore.

Parking: Don't drive into the Cinque Terre villages — ZTL zones, limited spots, €30–40/day. Park in La Spezia (Parcheggio Centro or Via Crispi, ~€15–18/day) and train in. Riomaggiore is 8 minutes, Monterosso 20 minutes.

Gear: Sturdy hiking shoes required — they check at trail entrances. No sandals or flip-flops. Pack water and snacks; limited shops between villages on the trail.
