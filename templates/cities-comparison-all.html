    Climate & Environment Summary

    Italy - Lake Como

    Winter Sunlight: The western shore towns (Cernobbio, Moltrasio, Laglio, Brienno, Argegno) receive significantly more winter sun due to flatter terrain and fewer mountain shadows. Eastern shore locations like Torno experience limited winter sunlight due to high mountains blocking light from the south and west.

    Climate: Mild Mediterranean-influenced climate with warm summers and cool winters. More rainfall than Spanish coastal regions, creating lush green landscapes. Average winter sunshine: 1.9-3 hours/day.

    Costa Blanca

    Sunshine: Excellent year-round sunshine with over 300 sunny days annually. Superior winter sun compared to Italian lakes. Warm, dry Mediterranean climate ideal for outdoor living.

    Landscape: More arid than Lake Como, with dramatic coastal cliffs, white sandy beaches, and mountain backdrops. Less lush vegetation but spectacular sea views.

    Costa Brava

    Climate: Mediterranean with good sunshine but cooler winters than Costa Blanca. More rainfall than Costa Blanca, resulting in greener landscapes with pine forests along the coast.

    Character: Rocky coves, artistic heritage, and traditional fishing villages. Barcelona proximity adds cultural dimension. More seasonal than Costa Blanca.
