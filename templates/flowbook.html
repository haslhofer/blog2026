FlowBook

The Reactive Notebook for Personal Productivity

What if your productivity tools worked like a Jupyter notebook—but instead of running Python code, the cells were live connectors to your calendar, email, tasks, and APIs, all streaming in continuous flow state? FlowBook is an iPython notebook-style tool where reactive, always-on cells update as your world changes.

This is an exploration of a product idea that synthesizes years of thinking about the future of knowledge work, AI-first interfaces, and the "flattening" of productivity APIs.

The Core Idea

Today's productivity stack is fragmented—note-taking apps to organize thoughts, email for communication, task managers to organize work, calendars to organize time. But when you look closer, these activities aren't that clear-cut. The boundaries between them are artificial, imposed by the apps themselves rather than the nature of the work.

FlowBook reimagines this stack as a single, programmable surface. Each cell in the notebook connects to a data source or action. Cells are reactive—when new email arrives, it triggers task extraction, which updates your priority queue. When a calendar event approaches, relevant context surfaces automatically. The notebook becomes the universal interface, and connectors abstract away whether the backend is Microsoft, Google, or indie.

Why Now?

The philosophy behind FlowBook is to work back from "continuous AI"—where an LLM reasons every 100 milliseconds across all modalities—rather than incrementally improving from 10 to 20 queries a day. Like anti-lock brakes: once sensors and actuators became continuous, driving became dramatically easier because it removed the need for a specific skill set. FlowBook applies the same principle to productivity— always-on reasoning removes the "skill" of productivity management itself.

Key Design Principles

AI Intermediation

  • AI intermediates everything that enters your world
  • It's the gatekeeper for all events & data
  • Routes, morphs objects, retrieves context automatically

Guided Actions

  • Lower mental effort—choose from options, don't compose
  • TikTok-style "nudges" to signal intent with one tap
  • AI guesses semantics: "remember", "explain", "add to-do"

Sandboxing & Control

  • PR model—LLM suggests changes, user commits them
  • Different "views" for different agents
  • Family gets full access, work agent gets filtered view

Real-time & Multiplayer

  • Figma/Fortnite-style collab-first model
  • ECS game-engine architecture for streaming artifacts
  • Layers: time, people, activities as sliceable dimensions

Product Directions

FlowBook Pro for Knowledge Workers: A desktop app targeting developers, analysts, and researchers who want a programmable "life dashboard" where cells react to real-time inputs. New email triggers task extraction, which updates the priority queue. Calendar events surface relevant context. The user stays in flow because the system handles the meta-work.

FlowBook Teams: An enterprise version where teams share reactive notebooks— live sales dashboards, on-call runbooks with real-time alerts, project trackers that pull from Jira or Linear automatically. The notebook becomes the team's shared operating surface.

The Vision

FlowBook is ultimately about building a true Memex—Vannevar Bush's vision of a device that stores and organizes all your books, records, and communications, allowing fast and flexible access. The challenge is breaking the stream of life into meaningful chunks, removing noise, making memories addressable, and promoting insights between short and long-term storage.

The notebook metaphor works because it's familiar to anyone who has used Jupyter or Observable. But instead of code cells producing outputs, FlowBook cells are live windows into your digital life—always updating, always ready, always in flow.
