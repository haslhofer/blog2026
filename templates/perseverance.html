Perseverance

On Core Values & The Outer Perimeter of What's Possible

Friends of our family recently asked me what my (our?) "core values" were. A question I don't get every day. What traits do I hold myself accountable to, what traits would I like to instill in my children as a strong foundation?

"Perseverance" is what I responded with.

I'm not sure what kind of picture this paints of me, but it was an honest answer. First what came to my mind. Would "Integrity" have been better? Honesty? Service? Empathy?

The Foundation of Success

If there's one thing I can point to about successes in my life is that it was about tirelessly persisting despite challenges and setbacks, informed by a clear view of what ultimate success could look like.

Running Toward a Dream

I had dreamed about running ultra-marathon distances for more than a year since finishing the LA marathon in 2023.

I reminded myself not to succumb to comfort when my alarm clock rang at 4:45am on Saturday morning to attempt my first 60k. I had not signed up for a race, and there wouldn't be any spectators along the route. I just wanted to achieve a goal important to me. I took my bag of gummy bears, a bottle of Gatorade, a credit card to pay for supplies along the route and started running from my house in the dark—knowing that it would be a hot day and that I'd better start going.

The Point

My sister later asked me what the "point" is of running a distance like that. I didn't have a good answer, apart from thinking that a life lived well is a life where I continuously expanded the outer perimeter of what I could have dreamed is possible.

Gerald Haslhofer · July 2024
