Moltrasio, Italy

Western Lake Como Shore Population: ~1,700 15-20 min to ISC

Serene retreat with elegant historic villas and excellent winter sun exposure

Excellent Winter Sun

Moltrasio's western shore position provides excellent winter sun exposure throughout the day. The village receives direct sunlight well into the afternoon during winter months, making it ideal for families who prioritize year-round natural light and outdoor living.

Overview

Character

Moltrasio is a serene, elegant village of approximately 1,700 residents on Lake Como's western shore. Famous for its historic villas including Villa Passalacqua and the former Villa Fontanelle (George Clooney's previous residence), Moltrasio attracts discerning buyers seeking tranquility and sophistication.

Location & Access

Located 11km north of Como along the western lakeshore road, Moltrasio offers easy access to the International School of Como (15-20 minutes). The village sits between Cernobbio to the south and Laglio to the north, making it an ideal central location on the prestigious western shore.

Lifestyle

Peaceful residential atmosphere with upscale properties, lakeside walking paths, and intimate trattorias. Popular with wealthy Milanese seeking weekend retreats. The village maintains authentic Italian character while offering refined amenities and exceptional privacy.

Historic Villas & Attractions

Villa Passalacqua

One of Lake Como's most celebrated historic estates, Villa Passalacqua dates to the 18th century and has been transformed into an ultra-luxury boutique hotel. The villa's terraced gardens, neoclassical architecture, and commanding lake views exemplify the grandeur that defines Moltrasio's architectural heritage.

Villa Fontanelle

This iconic villa gained international fame as George Clooney's Lake Como residence before he moved to nearby Laglio. The 18th-century estate features dramatic lakefront positioning and extensive gardens. While privately owned, the villa's presence contributes to Moltrasio's reputation as a destination for discerning international residents.

Church of San Martino

The historic Church of San Martino sits at the heart of Moltrasio's old village, featuring beautiful frescoes and traditional Lombard architecture. The church piazza serves as a gathering point for village celebrations and maintains the community's authentic Italian character.

Living in Moltrasio

Moltrasio represents refined lakeside living with a focus on privacy, natural beauty, and architectural heritage. The village maintains a quieter profile than neighboring Cernobbio, attracting families and couples who prioritize tranquility over amenities. Daily life centers around lakeside walks, local cafes, and the intimate village piazza.

The real estate market in Moltrasio emphasizes historic villas and upscale properties with limited new construction. Many estates date to the 18th and 19th centuries, offering original architectural details combined with modern renovations. Properties here command premium prices due to the western shore location, excellent sun exposure, and association with celebrated villas like Passalacqua and Fontanelle.

The village's excellent winter sun exposure makes it particularly appealing for year-round residents. Unlike eastern shore villages that lose afternoon light during winter months, Moltrasio receives direct sunlight throughout the day, enhancing outdoor living and natural warmth. Combined with the convenient 15-20 minute commute to ISC, Moltrasio offers an ideal balance of serenity, sunshine, and accessibility for international families.

Limited commercial development preserves Moltrasio's residential character. The village offers essential services—a small market, pharmacy, and several restaurants—but families will visit Como or Cernobbio for comprehensive shopping. This limitation reinforces the tranquil atmosphere while ensuring genuine village living rather than resort convenience.
