# Spain - Costa Blanca

**Location**: Mediterranean coast, Alicante province

## Overview

Mediterranean coast, Alicante province, year-round mild climate. Region offers some of the lowest international school fees in Europe while maintaining high-quality education standards.

## Top Priority Towns (Dramatic Seaside & Tranquil)

### 1. Javéa (Xàbia) - BEST MATCH ⭐

**Why It's the Best Match:**
- Dramatic coastline with cliffs at Cabo de la Nao and Cabo de San Antonio
- Rocky coves and dramatic headlands
- Tranquil, residential/expat-oriented atmosphere
- Less touristy than other coastal towns

**Key Areas to Explore:**

- [ ] Javéa old town and port area
- [ ] Arenal beach area
- [ ] Cabo de la Nao and coastal walks
- [ ] Residential neighborhoods with sea views

**International Schools:**

### 2. Altea - STRONG MATCH ⭐

**Why It's a Strong Match:**
- Dramatic hillside setting with white-washed old town perched above the Mediterranean
- Stunning mountain backdrop (Sierra de Bernia)
- More upscale and peaceful compared to neighboring towns
- Excellent scenic beauty

**Key Areas to Explore:**

- [ ] Altea old town (historic center)
- [ ] Beach promenade
- [ ] Cap Negret area
- [ ] Hillside residential areas with views

### 3. Calpe - MODERATE MATCH

**Why It's a Moderate Match:**
- Iconic Peñón de Ifach (332m limestone rock jutting from the sea)
- Very dramatic seaside landmark
- **Note**: Can be quite touristy in peak season

**Key Areas to Explore:**

- [ ] Old town area
- [ ] Peñón de Ifach natural park
- [ ] Beaches (Levante and Arenal-Bol)
- [ ] Residential areas away from tourist center

**International Schools:**

- See schools in Javéa and Altea areas (within reasonable driving distance)
- Also accessible: Denia schools (see Appendix)

---

## General Notes

- Schools typically follow British (GCSE/A-Level), American, or International Baccalaureate (IB) curricula
- Alicante and Costa Blanca region has comparatively lower international school fees than Madrid and other major Spanish cities
- School fees are among the lowest in Europe
- Most schools offer both Spanish/Valencian and international curricula
- Strong expat community presence in all coastal towns

## APPENDIX: Additional Costa Blanca Towns & Schools

### Towns to Avoid for Our Criteria

- **Benidorm** - Very large, extremely touristy high-rise resort city
- **Alicante** - Major city, not tranquil
- **Torrevieja** - Very touristy, flatter/less dramatic coastline

### Other Northern Costa Blanca Towns

#### Denia

- [ ] Coastal town with good access to nearby schools
- Located near Javéa
- Good port town atmosphere

#### Alfaz del Pi / Albir / La Nucia

- [ ] Family-friendly areas near Altea with good school access
- Inland from the coast but close proximity to beaches
